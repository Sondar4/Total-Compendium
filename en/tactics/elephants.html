Elephants and Chariots

Elephants and chariots where used before Dimas and Gestas died on the cross. Well, long before they were born. They are powerful artifacts. When well used, they'll give you decisive victories, like to Hannibal. When badly used, you'll have expensive loses, like Antiochus III.

As they are very expensive units, they have to be used wisey and they have to be protected from units that counte them, i.e., they have to be protected from enemy missiles and spears.

Finally, both chariots and elephants should always be on the move.

Elephants

Los elefantes son animales preciosos e inteligentes, pero como adversarios resultan aterradores. Son útiles para acabar con todo tipo de infantería (cuidado con las lanzas y mucho cuidado con las picas), pero hay que mantenerlos alejados de las flechas enemigas. Elephants are beautiful clever animals, but they can be horrific on the battlefield. They are useful to finish off all kinds of infantry (be careful with spears, be extremely careful with pikes), but they have to be away from enemy arrows.

Chariots

Chariot units are specially useful against light units. This is due to the fact that they do damage when they go through the units, but they got stuck in heavier units as in a swamp. For this reason, the best use you can give them is to walk them through the enemy's less armoured units, and if those units are expensive, better.

So it's very important which army you are facing: chariots do more damage when they go through the lines of the brave (but weakly armoured) lusitani than when they got stuck on a roman legionnaires unit. Combine this tips with the cavalry ones and there will be no men with harmless ankles.
